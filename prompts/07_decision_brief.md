# 🎯 Phase 7 — Decision Brief & Action Plan

> **Purpose:** Compile the entire NEXUS session into a single executive Decision Brief — with explicit confidence, hidden connections, frontier questions, and reversibility analysis.

---

## 📋 Prerequisites

- ✅ Master Directive loaded
- ✅ Phases 1-6 all complete

---

## 🎯 What This Phase Produces

The final NEXUS output is not a research summary. It is a **decision-ready document** with:

- One-paragraph CEO summary (60 seconds to read, with nuance)
- 5 key findings ranked by confidence
- The hidden connection that only emerged across phases
- The actionable insight, specific to YOUR role
- The frontier question that would change everything
- A confidence matrix table
- An action plan with reversibility analysis

---

## 📜 The Prompt (Copy Below)

```
[NEXUS Phase 7 — Decision Brief & Action Plan]

Synthesize everything from Phases 1-6 into a final Decision Brief.
Apply the Master Directive — be specific, no hedging, lead with the
hardest conclusions.

My role: [REPLACE WITH YOUR ROLE — e.g., "Enterprise Architect at a regulated bank",
"Hedge fund portfolio manager", "Policy advisor on AI regulation"]

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. THE ONE-PARAGRAPH CEO SUMMARY
   Explain this topic to a CEO who has 60 seconds.
   Include nuance — not just the headline.
   No disclaimers, no hedging language.

2. THE 5 KEY FINDINGS (ranked by confidence)
   For each, note:
   - The finding (1 sentence)
   - Confidence: HIGH / MODERATE / LOW / UNKNOWN
   - Which archetypes supported it
   - Which archetypes challenged it
   - Why this matters operationally

3. THE HIDDEN CONNECTION
   What non-obvious link emerged between findings — something that only
   becomes visible after running all 9 archetypes and 5 debates?
   This is often the most valuable output of the entire session.

4. THE ACTIONABLE INSIGHT
   Based on all the evidence, what should someone in MY role specifically
   do differently starting tomorrow?
   Be specific. Name the decision. Name the trade-off.

5. THE FRONTIER QUESTION
   The one question that, if answered, would change everything about how
   we understand this topic.
   Who could answer it? What evidence would they need?

6. CONFIDENCE MATRIX
   Produce a table:

   | Insight | Confidence | Evidence Base | What Would Change This |
   |---------|-----------|---------------|------------------------|

7. ACTION PLAN WITH REVERSIBILITY ANALYSIS
   For each recommended action:
   - The action (concrete, specific)
   - Reversibility: REVERSIBLE / PARTIALLY REVERSIBLE / IRREVERSIBLE
   - Cost of action if it's wrong
   - Cost of inaction if the analysis is right
   - Recommended sequence (which actions first?)

8. THE BRUTAL HONEST VERDICT
   In one paragraph: what is the strongest counterargument to your
   own recommendations?
   If you had to bet money on this analysis being wrong, where would
   you place the bet?

Format: structured executive brief, professional tone, no AI-speak.
This is the document I will use to make a real decision.
```

---

## 📤 Expected Output

Eight clearly-headed sections, professional executive-brief format. The Confidence Matrix should be a real table. The Action Plan must include reversibility classification per action.

---

## 💡 Tips

- **The Brutal Honest Verdict section (#8) is the most important.** It's where the analysis attacks itself one final time. If the AI cannot produce a strong counterargument to its own recommendations, the analysis is likely incomplete.
- **The Reversibility Analysis changes prioritization.** Reversible actions should be taken faster (low cost of error); irreversible actions should be delayed and stress-tested further.
- **The Frontier Question is often what you should research NEXT.** It's the unsolved question that would unlock the next layer of understanding.
