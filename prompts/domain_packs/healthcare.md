## 📊 Domain-Specific Data Sources & Evidence Standards

Weight: peer-reviewed clinical evidence (NEJM, JAMA, Lancet, BMJ), RCTs over observational studies, real-world post-market surveillance, multi-population validation (spectrum bias awareness), regulatory guidance documents.

Skeptical of: single-center studies, algorithm validation on training-distribution-similar test data, "AI outperforms doctors" headlines without scope, vendor-funded clinical studies.

---

## 🎚️ Confidence Calibration Notes

- **HIGH:** Multi-site RCT replication + post-market confirmation
- **MODERATE:** Single high-quality RCT + mechanistic plausibility
- **LOW:** Single-center or observational evidence
- **UNKNOWN:** Theoretical or pre-clinical claims

Be especially conservative — clinical AI has high stakes and over-confidence has caused real damage historically.
