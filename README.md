## 🚀 What is NEXUS?

NEXUS is a **7-phase, 9-archetype research method** that turns any AI chat into a structured expert panel for **executive decision-making**.

It is **inspired by Stanford's STORM** (NAACL 2024) but redesigned for a different purpose: while STORM optimizes for *article generation*, NEXUS optimizes for *decision-making under uncertainty*.

### 🎯 What NEXUS Adds Beyond STORM

| Capability | NEXUS Contribution |
|---|---|
| 🎭 **9 Sharply-Defined Archetypes** | Including Insider, Contrarian, Regulator, Futurist — perspectives single-shot research consistently misses |
| ⚔️ **Adversarial Dialectic Engine** | Forces structured debates between experts, not just parallel statements |
| 📊 **Explicit Confidence Grading** | Every claim graded: High / Moderate / Low / Unknown |
| 🔮 **Temporal Synthesis** | Past patterns + Present reality + Future scenarios with Best/Base/Worst cases |
| ⚠️ **Pre-Mortem & Counterfactual** | "How could this fail?" baked into the method |
| 🎨 **Domain-Tunable** | Pre-built domain packs for Finance, EA, Healthcare, AI Strategy, Investments |
| 🌐 **Polymath Mode (Optional)** | 12 domain lenses + ~50 mental models + analogical reasoning engine |
| 📱 **Method-Portable** | Runs in any LLM (Claude, GPT, Copilot, Gemini) — no code required |

## ⚡ 5-Minute Quick Start

1. **Read** `prompts/00_master_directive.md` and paste it into your AI chat
2. **Copy** prompts `01` through `07` from the `prompts/` directory, one at a time
3. **Replace** `[YOUR TOPIC]` with your real research question
4. **Read** the final Decision Brief that emerges

No software. No setup. Just structured thinking compressed into 7 prompts.

## 🧬 The 9 Expert Archetypes

| # | Archetype | What They Uniquely See |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 🛠️ **Practitioner** | Ground-truth realities, what actually works in the field |
| 2 | 🎓 **Academic** | Peer-reviewed evidence, theoretical foundations |
| 3 | 🤔 **Skeptic** | Hidden flaws, overhyped narratives |
| 4 | 💰 **Economist** | Money flows, incentive structures, unit economics |
| 5 | 📜 **Historian** | Repeating patterns, cyclical lessons |
| 6 | 🔮 **Futurist** | Second-order consequences, emerging trends |
| 7 | ⚖️ **Regulator** | Compliance, ethics, systemic risk |
| 8 | 🔥 **Contrarian** | The opposite view almost no one considers |
| 9 | 🕵️ **Insider** | What the industry knows but doesn't say publicly |

## 🌐 Polymath Mode (Optional)

For genuinely novel/breakthrough problems:
- **12 Domain Lenses** — Physics, Biology, Math, Economics, Psychology, History, Philosophy, CS, Art & Design, Systems Theory, Linguistics, Statistics
- **~50 Mental Models Library** — Munger-style latticework
- **Analogical Reasoning Engine** — finds structural patterns across domains

Three worked examples in `examples/` demonstrate Polymath Mode produces insights single-domain experts miss.

## 🆚 NEXUS vs STORM — Honest Positioning

NEXUS **stands on STORM's shoulders**. STORM proved multi-perspective AI research is structurally superior to single-shot prompting. NEXUS extends that insight from *article generation* into *executive decision-making* — adding adversarial debate, confidence grading, temporal scenarios, and domain tuning.

Full comparison in `docs/COMPARISON.md`.

## ⚠️ Honest Caveats

- **NEXUS is not magic.** ~25-30% of Polymath insights are available scattered in different literatures; the value is **forced systematic synthesis**.
- **The 12 lenses are not equal.** Tier 1 (Biology, History, Systems Theory, Math, Economics, Physics) consistently outperform Tier 3 (Linguistics, Art & Design).
- **Adversarial Dialectic Engine is the strongest differentiator vs STORM.**
- **Domain expertise still matters.** NEXUS amplifies expert thinking; it does not replace it.
