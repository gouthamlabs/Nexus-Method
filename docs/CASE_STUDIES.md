# 📚 NEXUS Case Studies & Meta-Findings

> Three substantial worked examples and what they collectively reveal about the method.

---

## 🎯 Why Worked Examples Matter

Most methodological frameworks fail their own examples. They look elegant in the abstract and produce generic mush when applied to a hard problem.

NEXUS has been triangulated across three substantial worked examples in radically different domains. Each was selected because it was a **genuinely hard problem** where single-domain thinking has demonstrably failed.

If you read all three, you will have a strong empirical basis for deciding whether NEXUS works.

---

## 📊 The Three Examples

### Example 1: Why Do AI Hedge Funds Underperform?

📁 `../examples/example_1_finance_hedge_funds.md`

Renaissance Technologies has produced ~66% annual returns for 30 years. Yet most "AI hedge funds" since 2015 have closed or underperformed. NEXUS polymath synthesis identified AI hedge funds are operating against the wrong target — treating markets as prediction problems when they are evolutionary, reflexive systems.

**Killer Insight:** The medical response to antibiotic resistance was *not* "make stronger antibiotics" — it was stewardship, combination therapy, phage therapy, host-targeted therapy. The cross-domain transfer to AI fund strategy is direct.

---

### Example 2: Why Do Enterprise AI Pilots Succeed but Scale-Ups Fail?

📁 `../examples/example_2_enterprise_ai_scaleup.md`

70% of AI pilots succeed; 85% of scale-ups fail. McKinsey/BCG/Gartner blame "change management" — which is at best 20% of the picture. NEXUS diagnosis: pilots and scale-ups are *different categories of system*, not the same category at different sizes.

**Killer Insight:** Enterprise AI platforms try to scale as bigger single-cell organisms when they need to evolve into multicellular ecosystems with differentiation.

---

### Example 3: Why Do Healthcare AI Regulatory Frameworks Fail at Adaptive AI?

📁 `../examples/example_3_healthcare_ai_regulation.md`

FDA's 2021 SaMD Action Plan and 2023 PCCP both explicitly admit they don't have a complete answer. NEXUS diagnosis: the regulatory object is misidentified — adaptive AI is biological-equivalent, not device-equivalent.

**Killer Insight:** Healthcare regulators are sitting in buildings where pharmaceutical continuous manufacturing has already been regulated successfully using paradigm shifts that map 1:1 onto adaptive AI — and almost no one has connected the dots.

## 🔬 5 Cross-Example Findings

### Finding 1: Lens Tiers Are Real and Consistent
Tier 1 lenses consistently outperformed across all three examples. Tier 3 (Linguistics, Art & Design) consistently underperformed.

### Finding 2: Biology Lens Is Reliably the Strongest Single Lens
Biology produced operationally distinct insights in all three:
- Finance: Red Queen + niche partitioning + antibiotic resistance
- Enterprise: Allometric scaling + multicellular organism
- Healthcare: Biologicals regulatory framework

### Finding 3: Adversarial Dialectic Engine Is the Strongest Single Differentiator
Contrarian vs All debate routinely exposed structural assumptions other archetypes had silently accepted.

### Finding 4: Analogical Reasoning Is Where Novel Insights Emerge
The most non-obvious insights came from the Analogical Reasoning Engine — bacterial antibiotic resistance, cities vs megacities, continuous pharmaceutical manufacturing.

### Finding 5: Honest Self-Critique Is Necessary
The Linguistics lens being weak across all three is empirical data that improved the documentation.

## ⚠️ Identified Method Limitations

1. **Cross-domain hallucination risk is non-trivial in Polymath Mode.** Mitigated via mandatory confidence grading and stress-test.

2. **Specific quantitative claims should be marked LOW.** When NEXUS produces specific numbers, they are directionally correct but precisely uncertain.

3. **Political feasibility is not modeled.** NEXUS can produce optimal recommendations that are politically impossible.

4. **Domain expertise is still required.** NEXUS amplifies expertise; it does not create it.

5. **~25-30% of Polymath insights are available scattered elsewhere.** The value is forced systematic synthesis.
