# 🌐 Polymath Mode — When to Invoke

> Comprehensive guide to NEXUS's optional cross-domain reasoning layer.

---

## 🎯 What Polymath Mode Adds

| Component | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **12 Domain Lenses** | Force the problem through 12 fundamentally different disciplinary lenses |
| **~50 Mental Models Library** | Munger-style latticework of portable thinking tools |
| **Analogical Reasoning Engine** | Cross-domain structural-isomorphism search and solution translation |

---

## 🔬 The Triangulation Finding — Lenses Are Not Equal

| Tier | Lenses | Empirical Behavior |
|---|---|---|
| **🥇 Tier 1 — Always Run** | Biology, History, Systems Theory, Mathematics, Economics, Physics | Consistently produced actionable insights across all three examples |
| **🥈 Tier 2 — Often Productive** | Psychology, Statistics, Computer Science, Philosophy | Frequently sharp insights, higher variance |
| **🥉 Tier 3 — Inconsistent** | Linguistics, Art & Design | Clever framing, weak operational prescriptions |

**Default recommendation:** Always run Tier 1. Add Tier 2 if Tier 1 hasn't converged. Add Tier 3 only when problem has semantic or compositional dimensions.

---

## ✅ When to Invoke

Use Polymath Mode when **at least three** are true:

- Problem is genuinely novel and standard frameworks have failed
- Cross-domain synthesis is required
- You need breakthrough thinking rather than incremental optimization
- Single-discipline experts have produced incompatible diagnoses
- You can accept higher cross-domain confabulation risk for novel insight
- The decision is strategic (5+ year horizon)
- You have domain expertise to validate cross-domain analogies

---

## ⛔ When NOT to Invoke

- Decision is routine or operational
- Time-constrained (<30 min decision)
- Domain is well-mapped and well-understood
- You cannot personally validate cross-domain analogies
- Audience will reject "TED talk synthesis"
- You are trying to look smart rather than be right

---

## 📊 Honest Acknowledgment

**~25-30% of Polymath Mode insights are available scattered in different literatures.** The value Polymath Mode adds is **forced systematic synthesis** — not generation of insights that were structurally impossible.

A domain expert who reads across fields and applies mental models systematically can produce 80-90% of Polymath Mode's output without the framework. NEXUS-P provides structure that makes this systematic and replicable.

---

## ⚠️ Risks

### 1. Cross-Domain Hallucination
LLMs are worse at multi-domain synthesis than single-domain analysis.
**Mitigation:** Mandatory confidence grading. Explicit verify-with-expert flags. Step 7 stress test.

### 2. Pseudo-Erudition (The TED Talk Problem)
Outputs sound impressive while operationally shallow.
**Mitigation:** Every analogical insight must translate into a concrete testable action. If it cannot, discard.

### 3. Decision Paralysis
12 lenses × 50 models × adversarial debate can overload the decision process.
**Mitigation:** Polymath Mode is opt-in. Default to Tier 1 lenses only.

### 4. Dilettantism Detection
Real domain experts spot shallow domain knowledge instantly.
**Mitigation:** Each lens prompt asks for expert-grade analogies, not consumer-grade intuitions.

## 🔧 How to Combine with Standard NEXUS

```
1. Phases 1-3 (standard NEXUS)
   - Phase 1: Context Grounding
   - Phase 2: 9-Archetype Simulation
   - Phase 3: Adversarial Dialectic Engine

2. If standard NEXUS has not produced clarity, invoke Polymath Mode:
   - prompts/polymath/01_twelve_domain_lenses.md
   - prompts/polymath/02_mental_models_library.md
   - prompts/polymath/03_analogical_reasoning_engine.md ← killer step

3. Phases 4-7 (standard NEXUS — now informed by polymath insights)
   - Phase 4: Evidence Weighting & Confidence Grading
   - Phase 5: Blind Spot & Counterfactual Analysis
   - Phase 6: Temporal Synthesis
   - Phase 7: Decision Brief
```

Polymath insights must survive the same Phase 4 evidence-grading and Phase 5 blind-spot scrutiny.

---

## 🎯 Bottom Line

Polymath Mode is for problems where single-domain experts have demonstrably failed. For everything else, standard NEXUS is sufficient and faster.

Use it sparingly. Use it honestly. Verify the cross-domain claims.
